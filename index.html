Shaping your impression of shapes

Our understanding of the world coalesces around shapes:

We are perpetually surrounded by and defined through both abstract and tangible forms. In fact, are not the paradigms we create to explain our systems, just another Geometric? The constructs of order and disorder and the struggle that exists between them--a recurring theme in the innately human fields of art, philosophy, history, politics-- are nothing more than chaotic shapes bouncing against each other producing and molding an outcome. Ultimately is the source of humanity's fascination and infatuation with shapes a result of our own place in the universe: chaotic colliding objects, which through interaction give rise to new diversified discourses and engendered the unpredictable kaleidoscope of new geometrics? Are shapes the essence of our world and the foundation of our reality, or are shapes artificial figments of imagination used to define that which resist definition ? Yet, before we begin to answer that question, should we not immerse ourselves in the age old question: what is a shape, and what are the implications or ramifications of attempting to define such a relative concept?
